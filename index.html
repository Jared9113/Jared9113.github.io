Poem III of V

You Are My Home

I have searched through crowded cities, Through mountains, shores, and open skies, For somewhere that felt like belonging, And found it in your eyes. You are the place I return to When the world feels cold and wide, You are my shelter, my stillness, The warmth I feel inside. Not a building nor a city, Not a room with walls and doors, But the feeling of you beside me — That is what I call home, what I live for.
— You are my favourite place
Poem IV of V

A Thousand Times Over

If I were given a thousand lives And asked to choose again each time, I would find my way back to you Through every space and every rhyme. Across a thousand different worlds, In every shape and form I wear, My soul would recognise your soul And know that it belonged right there. For you are not just who I love In this one brief and precious life, You are the answer to the question My heart has always asked of life.
— In every life, always you
Poem V of V

Still

When the years have turned to decades And our hair has gone to grey, I will still reach for your hand At the beginning of each day. I will still be lost inside your laughter, Still amazed that you are mine, Still grateful for the ordinary evenings Spent with you — the most extraordinary sign. For love is not a feeling that fades quietly, Not with you, not with what we've built, It only deepens, roots grow longer, A garden tended without guilt. I loved you then, I love you now, And when forever comes to call, I will love you still — completely, Most impossibly, and above all.
— Now, always, and still
